üìö Background & Principles

A voucher system is a set of procedures and documents used to control cash disbursements and ensure that payments are made only for authorized transactions. It creates a paper trail that verifies each payment is proper before checks are issued.

Core Principle: A voucher is a "permission slip" for payment. Before any check is written, all supporting documents must be gathered, verified, and approved‚Äîcreating a complete audit trail.
üí° Key Insight: Think of a voucher like a permission slip for money. Before you pay ANYONE, you need proof that the goods were ordered, received, and the invoice is correct. No voucher, no payment!

üîë Key Concepts

Voucher

A document authorizing a cash payment, containing all supporting documentation for the transaction.

Purchase Requisition

A request to the purchasing department to order needed goods or services.

Purchase Order

A formal document sent to a vendor authorizing a purchase at specified terms.

Receiving Report

A document confirming that goods were received and inspecting their condition and quantity.

Invoice

A vendor's bill requesting payment, showing amounts owed for goods or services.

Voucher Register

A journal used to record all approved vouchers before payment.

Document What It Verifies Key Fields to Match

üö´ Common Misconceptions & Professional Tips

‚ùå Misconception 1: "A voucher is just an invoice approval."

‚úÖ Reality: A voucher includes the invoice PLUS all supporting documents (PO, receiving report). It's a complete file, not just an approval stamp.
‚ùå Misconception 2: "Small purchases don't need a voucher."

‚úÖ Reality: All cash disbursements should be documented. Petty cash has its own system; larger purchases need full voucher documentation.
‚ùå Misconception 3: "If the invoice matches the PO, we can pay."

‚úÖ Reality: Always compare invoice to RECEIVING REPORT too. What if the vendor shipped only 50 widgets but billed for 100?
üí° Professional Tip #1: Match Purchase Order ‚Üí Receiving Report ‚Üí Invoice before approving any voucher.
üí° Professional Tip #2: Use prenumbered vouchers to prevent duplicate payments and identify missing documents.
üí° Professional Tip #3: Separate the functions of ordering, receiving, and paying to maintain internal control.

üìñ Glossary

Voucher System

A formal document authorizing a vendor to supply goods at specified terms.

Receiving Report

A document confirming goods were received, inspecting quantity and condition.

The process of verifying that all documents are correct and match before authorizing payment.
